Atrium is a controller for the servers you already run. Point it at your own
machines and manage the whole stack from your phone, without a middleman
account and without anything phoning home.

It is a controller, not a media player. Atrium browses and manages your media
servers; it does not stream video.

Supported services:

* Sonarr and Radarr - library, queue, wanted, history, blocklist, system status
  and the full settings tree
* Prowlarr - indexers, search and grab
* Bazarr - subtitle status, search and download
* Seerr (Overseerr and Jellyseerr) - discover, request and manage requests
* Tautulli - active streams, history, statistics and users
* qBittorrent and SABnzbd - transfers and queue control
* Jellyfin, Emby and Plex - library browsing, item detail, resume rows and
  now-playing sessions
* Glances - CPU, memory, GPU and disk usage of the host

Features:

* Multiple profiles, so a phone can carry more than one stack
* Every instance holds a local and an external URL, and Atrium picks the right
  one for the network you are on
* A dashboard of at-a-glance widgets you can reorder: active downloads, now
  streaming, upcoming releases, recently added, recently downloaded, requests
  and server info
* Profile import and export
* Material 3 Expressive design with dynamic color, custom palettes, light and
  dark themes
* An optional biometric lock

Privacy:

Atrium talks only to the servers you configure. There is no analytics, no
telemetry, no advertising and no account. Credentials are held in encrypted
storage on the device.
